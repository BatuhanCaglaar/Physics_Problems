# Problem 1: Sound Wave Propagation

**Given:**
- Frequency: $f = 440 \, \text{Hz}$
- Speed of sound in air: $v_{\text{air}} = 343 \, \text{m/s}$
- Speed of sound in water: $v_{\text{water}} = 1482 \, \text{m/s}$

---

## Solution:
The wavelength ($\lambda$) is determined by the formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

**a) Wavelength in Air:**
$$\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{v_{\text{air}}}{f} = \frac{343}{440} \approx \mathbf{0.780 \, \text{m}}$$

**b) Wavelength in Water:**
The frequency of a wave is determined by its source and remains constant when the wave changes medium.
$$\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{v_{\text{water}}}{f} = \frac{1482}{440} \approx \mathbf{3.368 \, \text{m}}$$

**Answer:** The wavelength is approximately **0.780 m** in air and **3.368 m** in water.

# Section 0 — Mathematical Foundations
## 3. Proportionality

We use the **Universal Law of Gravitation**:

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

---

## Step-by-Step Calculation

Substitute the new values into the formula:

$$F' = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

### 1) Simplify the numerator:
$$\frac{1}{2}m_1 \cdot \frac{1}{2}m_2 = \frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2$$

### 2) Simplify the denominator:
$$(2r)^2 = 4r^2$$

### 3) Combine them:
$$F' = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2} = \frac{1}{16} \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$

---

## Final Answer

The force $F$ decreases by a factor of **16**.

$$F' = \frac{1}{16} F$$

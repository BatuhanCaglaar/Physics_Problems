## 3. Proportionality (Universal Law of Gravitation)

###  Problem Statement
Using the formula $F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$, determine how the force $F$ changes if:
1. The distance $r$ is **doubled** ($r' = 2r$).
2. Both masses $m_1$ and $m_2$ are **halved** ($m' = m/2$).

---

###  My Logic (Explanation for Class)
"In physics, we often look at how variables scale. Since the force is **directly proportional** to the masses and **inversely proportional** to the square of the distance, I substituted the new values into the original equation to find the scaling factor."

---

###  Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step 1: Write the new parameters**
- New masses: $m_1' = \frac{1}{2}m_1$ and $m_2' = \frac{1}{2}m_2$
- New distance: $r' = 2r$

**Step 2: Substitute into the formula**
$$F' = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

**Step 3: Simplify the fractions**
- The numerator becomes: $\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2$
- The denominator becomes: $4r^2$

**Step 4: Combine everything**
$$F' = \frac{1/4}{4} \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right) = \frac{1}{16} F$$

---

###  Final Answer
The gravitational force decreases by a factor of **16**.

---
*Presentation Tip: Mention that the distance squared ($r^2$) is the most sensitive part of the equation. Doubling the distance alone would have decreased the force by 4 times, but halving the masses adds another 4 times decrease ($4 \times 4 = 16$).*

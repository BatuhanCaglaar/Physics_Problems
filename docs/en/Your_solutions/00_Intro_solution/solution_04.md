## 4. Rearranging Formulas (Pendulum Motion)

### Problem Statement
Given the formula for the period of a simple pendulum, rearrange it to solve for the acceleration due to gravity ($g$).

---

### Mathematical Derivation
**Original Formula:**
$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

**Step-by-Step Rearrangement:**

1. **Isolate the radical:** Divide both sides by $2\pi$
$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

2. **Remove the square root:** Square both sides of the equation
$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

3. **Invert the equation:** Take the reciprocal of both sides
$$\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2} = \frac{g}{L}$$

4. **Isolate $g$:** Multiply both sides by $L$
$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

---

### Final Result
The formula for gravity based on pendulum motion is:
$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

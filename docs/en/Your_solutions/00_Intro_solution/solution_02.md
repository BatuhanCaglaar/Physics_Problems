## 2. Systems of Equations

We are given a system of two linear equations:

$$
\begin{cases}
2x + 3y = 12 \\
x - y = 1
\end{cases}
$$

---

## Method: Substitution

### 1) Express $x$ in terms of $y$
From the second equation:
$$x = y + 1$$

### 2) Substitute into the first equation
Substitute $x$ into $2x + 3y = 12$:
$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$

### 3) Solve for $y$
Expand the parentheses:
$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$
Combine like terms:
$$5y + 2 = 12$$
Subtract 2 from both sides:
$$5y = 10 \implies y = 2$$

### 4) Solve for $x$
Using $x = y + 1$:
$$x = 2 + 1 = 3$$

---

## Final Answer (Summary)
The values that satisfy both equations are:
$$x = 3, \quad y = 2$$

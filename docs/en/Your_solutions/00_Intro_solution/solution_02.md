# Section 0 — Mathematical Foundations
## 2. Systems of Equations

###  Problem Statement
Find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy:
1. $2x + 3y = 12$
2. $x - y = 1$

---

###  My Logic (Explanation for Class)
"To solve this, I used the **Substitution Method**. The second equation is much simpler, so I rearranged it to isolate $x$. Once I found $x$ in terms of $y$, I plugged it back into the first equation to reduce it to a single variable."

---

###  Step-by-Step Solution

**Step 1: Isolate $x$ from Equation (2)**
$$x = y + 1$$

**Step 2: Substitute into Equation (1)**
$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$

**Step 3: Simplify and solve for $y$**
$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$
$$5y + 2 = 12$$
$$5y = 10 \implies y = 2$$

**Step 4: Find $x$ using the value of $y$**
$$x = 2 + 1 = 3$$

---

###  Final Answer
The solution to the system is:
**$x = 3, \quad y = 2$**

---
*Note: We can verify this by checking: $2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12$. The result is correct!*

# Problem 4: Comparison of $F_e$ and $F_g$ in Hydrogen

**Given:**
- $r = 5.3 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{m}$
- $q_e = q_p = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}$
- $m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg}$, $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg}$

---

## Solution:
1. **Electric Force ($F_e$):**
   $$F_e = k \frac{e^2}{r^2} = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2} \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{N}$$

2. **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):**
   $$F_g = G \frac{m_p m_e}{r^2} = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.11 \times 10^{-31})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2} \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \, \text{N}$$

3. **Ratio ($F_e/F_g$):**
   $$\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx \frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}$$

**Answer:**
The electric force is vastly stronger than the gravitational force by a factor of approximately **$2.27 \times 10^{39}$**.

# Problem 2: Electric Potential at the Center

**Given:**
- Square side: $a = 1.0 \, \text{m}$
- Charges: $q_1 = +1\text{C}, q_2 = -2\text{C}, q_3 = +3\text{C}, q_4 = -4\text{C}$
- Distance to center: $r = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \, \text{m}$

---

## Solution:
Electric potential ($V$) is a scalar quantity. The total potential at the center is the algebraic sum of the potentials from each charge:
$$V_{total} = \sum k \frac{q_i}{r} = \frac{k}{r} (q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + q_4)$$

Substitute the values:
$$V_{total} = \frac{k}{\sqrt{2}/2} (1 - 2 + 3 - 4)$$
$$V_{total} = \frac{2k}{\sqrt{2}} (-2) = -2\sqrt{2}k$$

Using $k \approx 8.99 \times 10^9 \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$:
$$V_{total} = -2 \cdot 1.414 \cdot 8.99 \times 10^9 \approx -2.54 \times 10^{10} \, \text{V}$$

**Answer:**
The electric potential at the center is **$-2\sqrt{2}k$** or approximately **$-2.54 \times 10^{10} \, \text{V}$**.

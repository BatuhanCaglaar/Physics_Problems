# Problem 3: Equilibrium Position for $q_3$

**Given:**
- $q_1 = +4 \, \text{C}$ at $x = 0$
- $q_2 = +9 \, \text{C}$ at $x = 2 \, \text{m}$
- $q_3 = +1 \, \text{C}$ placed at distance $x$ from $q_1$.

---

## Solution:
For equilibrium, the forces from $q_1$ and $q_2$ on $q_3$ must be equal in magnitude and opposite in direction:
$$F_{13} = F_{23} \implies k \frac{q_1 q_3}{x^2} = k \frac{q_2 q_3}{(2-x)^2}$$
$$\frac{4}{x^2} = \frac{9}{(2-x)^2}$$

Take the square root of both sides:
$$\frac{2}{x} = \frac{3}{2-x}$$
$$2(2-x) = 3x \implies 4 - 2x = 3x \implies 4 = 5x$$
$$x = 0.8 \, \text{m}$$

**Answer:**
The equilibrium position is **0.8 meters** away from the $+4 \, \text{C}$ charge.

# Problem 8: Centripetal Acceleration of the Earth

Calculate the acceleration of a person at the Earth's equator.
- **Radius ($R$):** $6378 \text{ km} = 6.378 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
- **Rotation Period ($T$):** $24 \text{ hours} = 86,400 \text{ s}$

---

## 1) Calculation of Angular Velocity ($\omega$)
$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{86400} \approx 7.272 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$$

## 2) Calculation of Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$)
$$a_c = \omega^2 R$$
$$a_c = (7.272 \times 10^{-5})^2 \cdot (6.378 \times 10^6)$$
$$a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$$

**Result:** The acceleration is $0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$, which is significantly smaller than the acceleration due to gravity ($g \approx 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$).

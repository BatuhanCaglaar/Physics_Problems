## 2. Range Optimization

### Analytical Proof
The range formula for a projectile on level ground is:
$$R(\theta) = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

To find the maximum range, we take the derivative with respect to $\theta$ and set it to zero:
$$\frac{dR}{d\theta} = \frac{v_0^2}{g} \cdot \cos(2\theta) \cdot 2 = 0$$

Since $v_0^2/g \neq 0$:
$$\cos(2\theta) = 0$$
$$2\theta = 90^\circ \implies \theta = 45^\circ$$

**Conclusion:**
The maximum range is achieved at a launch angle of **$45^\circ$** because $\sin(2\theta)$ reaches its maximum value of 1 at this point.

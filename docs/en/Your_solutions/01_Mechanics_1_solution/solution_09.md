# Problem 9: Momentum Comparison ($p = m \cdot v$)

We compare the momentum of a fly and a tennis ball.

---

## 1) Calculations
- **Fly:**
  - $m_1 = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
  - $v_1 = 10 \text{ m/s}$
  - $p_1 = 0.002 \cdot 10 = \mathbf{0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$

- **Tennis Ball:**
  - $m_2 = 60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$
  - $v_2 = 1 \text{ m/s}$
  - $p_2 = 0.060 \cdot 1 = \mathbf{0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$

## 2) Result
The **tennis ball** has greater momentum ($0.06 > 0.02$). Although the fly is 10 times faster, the ball is 30 times heavier, making its momentum larger.

# Problem 5: Momentum Conservation and Energy Dissipation

A $70 \text{ kg}$ runner moving at $3 \text{ m/s}$ jumps onto a $140 \text{ kg}$ stationary cart. We analyze the final velocity and the conservation of kinetic energy.

---

## 1) Theory: Conservation of Linear Momentum

In any collision where no external horizontal forces act on the system, the total linear momentum ($\vec{P}$) is conserved:
$$\vec{P}_{initial} = \vec{P}_{final}$$

Since the runner and the cart move together after the jump, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**.

---

## 2) Calculation of Final Speed ($V_f$)

**Given:**
- Mass of runner ($m_r$): $70 \text{ kg}$
- Initial speed of runner ($v_r$): $3 \text{ m/s}$
- Mass of cart ($m_c$): $140 \text{ kg}$
- Initial speed of cart ($v_c$): $0 \text{ m/s}$

**The momentum equation:**
$$m_r v_r + m_c v_c = (m_r + m_c) V_f$$

Substitute the values:
$$(70 \cdot 3) + (140 \cdot 0) = (70 + 140) \cdot V_f$$
$$210 = 210 \cdot V_f$$

Solving for $V_f$:
$$V_f = 1 \text{ m/s}$$

---

## 3) Kinetic Energy Analysis

Is kinetic energy conserved? Let's compare the energy before and after the interaction.

**Initial Kinetic Energy ($K_i$):**
$$K_i = \frac{1}{2} m_r v_r^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2$$
$$K_i = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \text{ J}$$

**Final Kinetic Energy ($K_f$):**
$$K_f = \frac{1}{2} (m_r + m_c) V_f^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2$$
$$K_f = 105 \text{ J}$$

---

## 4) Discussion and Conclusion

**Is kinetic energy conserved?**
**No.** $K_i \neq K_f$. There is a loss of $210 \text{ J}$ ($66.7\%$ of the initial energy).

**Explanation:**
In a perfectly inelastic collision, kinetic energy is **never conserved**. The "missing" $210 \text{ Joules}$ is converted into other forms of energy, such as:
- **Internal Energy:** Heat generated by the friction between the runner's shoes and the cart.
- **Deformation:** Work done during the physical impact of landing.
- **Sound:** The noise of the impact.

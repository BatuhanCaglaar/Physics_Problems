# Problem 6: Electromagnetic Wave Equation

**Given:** $E_y(x,t) = 100 \sin(10^7 x - \omega t)$

---

## Solution:
1. **Direction of Propagation:** The argument $(kx - \omega t)$ indicates motion in the **$+x$ direction**.
2. **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** $k = 10^7 \implies \lambda = 2\pi/k = 2\pi \times 10^{-7} \approx \mathbf{6.28 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}}$.
3. **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** $\omega = c \cdot k = (3 \times 10^8) \cdot 10^7 = \mathbf{3 \times 10^{15} \, \text{rad/s}}$.
4. **Magnetic Field ($B_z$):** $B_0 = E_0/c = 100 / (3 \times 10^8) \approx 3.33 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}$.
   Since $\vec{E}$ is in $y$ and propagation is in $x$, $\vec{B}$ must be in $z$:
   $$B_z(x,t) = \mathbf{3.33 \times 10^{-7} \sin(10^7 x - 3 \times 10^{15} t) \, \text{T}}$$

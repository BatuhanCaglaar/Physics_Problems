# Problem 1: Electric Flux through a Sphere

**Given:**
- Point charge ($q$): $+2 \, \text{C}$
- Radius ($r$): $1 \, \text{m}$

---

## Solution:
According to Gauss's Law, the total electric flux ($\Phi_E$) through any closed surface is equal to the net charge enclosed divided by the permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$):
$$\Phi_E = \frac{q_{enclosed}}{\epsilon_0}$$

Note that the radius of the sphere does not affect the total flux as long as the charge is inside.
Using $\epsilon_0 \approx 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)$:
$$\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}} \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \, \text{V}\cdot\text{m}$$

**Answer:**
The electric flux is **$2.26 \times 10^{11} \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$**.

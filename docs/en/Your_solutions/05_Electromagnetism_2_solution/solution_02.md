# Problem 2: Magnetic Field Between Two Wires

**Given:**
- Separation ($d$): $10 \, \text{cm} = 0.1 \, \text{m}$
- Currents ($I_1, I_2$): $5 \, \text{A}$ (Opposite directions)
- Point: Midway ($r = d/2 = 0.05 \, \text{m}$)

---

## Solution:
Using the Right-Hand Rule:
1. Wire 1 (Current "Up"): Magnetic field at midway points **into** the page.
2. Wire 2 (Current "Down"): Magnetic field at midway points also points **into** the page.
Since both fields point in the same direction, they add up:
$$B_{total} = B_1 + B_2 = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} + \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} = \frac{\mu_0 I}{\pi r}$$

Substitute $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A}$:
$$B_{total} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \cdot 5}{\pi \cdot 0.05} = \frac{20 \times 10^{-7}}{0.05} = 4 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{T}$$

**Answer:**
The magnetic field magnitude is **$40 \, \mu\text{T}$** and the direction is **into the page** (perpendicular to the plane of the wires).
